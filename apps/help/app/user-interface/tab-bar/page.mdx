A diagram can hold many boards, and the **tab bar** is how you move between them. Each tab is its own canvas; the bar shows them in a row so the whole diagram is one click wide.

<Figure caption="A row of tab pills along the bottom: the active board, a couple of others, a collapsible folder, and the + button to add more.">
  <TabBar />
</Figure>

## What you can do here

- **Switch** between boards by clicking a tab.
- **Add** a new tab with the **+** button, blank or from a template.
- **Manage** a tab by right-clicking it (a [context menu](/help/user-interface/context-menus/)): rename, duplicate, lock, or delete.
- **Organise** related tabs into one-level collapsible folders when a diagram grows.

For the depth, see [Tabs](/help/tabs/) — this bar is just the control surface for them.
