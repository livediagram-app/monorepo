## A running record of your diagram

The Activity Panel is a floating, movable panel that keeps a list of every change made to the tab you are looking at. Each line tells you who made the change and when, so you always have a clear picture of how your diagram came to look the way it does.

<Figure caption="The Activity Panel lists recent changes with who, what, and when.">
  <ActivityOverview />
</Figure>

## Opening and moving it

Open the panel from its dock button. By default it sits in the bottom-left corner. You can drag it anywhere on the screen, and you can minimise it back to a button to keep the canvas clear. This is the same handy floating-panel behaviour you already get with the Explorer and the Palette.

## Always up to date, always there

The history is saved with the diagram, so it survives reloading the page, signing out, and re-sharing the diagram with others. It also updates as you work: when other people edit the same diagram, their changes appear in the list in real time.

Everyone can see the history, including guests and anyone viewing through a share link. Only people who are allowed to edit get the Undo, Redo, and Revert controls.

<Tip>
  The panel also shows a small "Saving…" or "Not saved" status, so you can tell at a glance that
  your work is safely stored.
</Tip>
