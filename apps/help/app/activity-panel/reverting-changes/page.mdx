## What revert does

Revert cancels one specific past change from the history, wherever it sits in the list, and leaves everything that came after it untouched.

<Figure caption="Reverting one past entry cancels just that change while the later rows stay.">
  <RevertStep />
</Figure>

## How it differs from undo

This is different from [Undo](/help/activity-panel/undo/), which only peels back your most recent change. Revert lets you reach into the list and cancel a single entry on its own. That is what makes the history safe to use when several people are editing at once: you can take back one change without unwinding everyone else's work.

## Reverting an entry

<Step n={1}>Open the Activity Panel and find the change you want to cancel.</Step>

<Step n={2}>Hover over that entry in the list.</Step>

<Step n={3}>Click its Revert button.</Step>

The reverted entry is removed from the list rather than adding a new "undo" line, which keeps the history tidy and easy to read.

<Note>Revert is hidden on locked tabs and for people who are only viewing the diagram.</Note>
