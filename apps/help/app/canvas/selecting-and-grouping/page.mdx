Diagrams rarely come together one shape at a time. Once a few elements are on the canvas you will want to move several at once, keep related pieces together, and make a row of shapes share the same look. livediagram gives you three tools for exactly that: selecting many elements, grouping them, and the format painter. None of them need a sign-in, so you can [open a canvas](/new) and try them straight away.

## Selecting more than one

The quickest way to grab several elements is the marquee: drag an empty patch of canvas and a selection rectangle sweeps up everything it touches. You can also build a selection click by click, adding and removing elements as you go. Either way, the selection becomes a single thing you can move, copy, or restyle in one go. See [Marquee and multi-select](/help/canvas/selecting-and-grouping/multi-select/) for every way to gather a selection.

## Keeping elements together

When a set of elements belongs together, turn the selection into a [group](/help/canvas/selecting-and-grouping/groups/). A group moves, copies, and selects as one unit, so a labelled box and its caption, or a cluster that makes up a single diagram component, never drift apart by accident. You can ungroup again whenever you need to fine-tune the pieces inside.

## Copying a look

Once you have styled one element just right, the [format painter](/help/canvas/selecting-and-grouping/using-the-format-painter/) copies that look onto others in a couple of clicks, so a whole diagram can share one consistent style without re-setting each colour and border by hand.

<Tip>
  Marquee selection pairs well with the format painter: select a row of shapes, then paint a styled
  element's look across all of them at once.
</Tip>
