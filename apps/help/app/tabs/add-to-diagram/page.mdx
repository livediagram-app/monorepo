Sometimes a tab you built in one diagram belongs in another, a reference board, a template flow, or a section you want to carry over. **Add to Diagram** copies the active tab into another diagram you own, without leaving the editor.

## Copying a tab across

<Step n={1}>Open the active tab's ellipsis menu and expand the **Organise** section.</Step>
<Step n={2}>Choose **Add to Diagram**.</Step>
<Step n={3}>Pick the destination diagram from your list. The tab is copied into it.</Step>

The option is only available when you actually have somewhere to copy to: if this is your only diagram, **Add to Diagram** is greyed out until you create another.

## What gets copied

Add to Diagram makes a copy, so the original tab stays exactly where it is. The destination diagram gets the tab's elements and layout as a new tab of its own. From there it is an ordinary tab in that diagram: rename it, fold it into a [tab folder](/help/tabs/tab-folders/), or restyle it without affecting the source.

<Tip>
  Building the same starter board again and again? Keep a master copy in one diagram and use **Add
  to Diagram** to drop it into each new project, then tailor it from there.
</Tip>
