**Shared with you** collects the diagrams other people have handed you, so a link a colleague sent last week is still easy to find this week. It sits in the Explorer's **Quick find** section, just below [Recent](/help/explorer/recent/), and shows a count of how many diagrams are waiting.

## How a diagram gets here

When you open a [share link](/help/collaboration/sharing/) that someone sent you, that diagram is remembered and added to **Shared with you**. You do not have to do anything extra: opening the link once is enough for it to show up here afterwards. Until you have opened a shared link, the section stays empty, with a prompt explaining that anything shared with you will land here.

## Working with shared diagrams

- **Reopen** a shared diagram by clicking its row, no need to dig the original link out of your messages.
- You can **dismiss** a diagram from the list when you are done with it. That only removes it from your Shared with you view; it does not affect the diagram or anyone else's access.
- Your access follows the share link's permissions, so whether you can edit or only view depends on how the owner shared it.

<Tip>
  Shared diagrams stay separate from **My Work**: they are other people's diagrams you have access
  to, not your own library, so they never clutter your folders.
</Tip>
