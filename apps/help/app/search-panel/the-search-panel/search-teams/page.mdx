When you are signed in and belong to one or more [teams](/help/collaboration/teams/), the [search panel](/help/search-panel/the-search-panel/) reaches into them as well, so team work is as findable as your own.

## What the Teams group matches

A single **Teams** group gathers three kinds of match:

- **Teams** themselves, by name. Picking one jumps to that team's page.
- **Team folders** shared inside a team.
- **Team diagrams** shared inside a team.

Team folders and diagrams are shown with an **"in &lt;team&gt;"** suffix, so when a name appears in more than one place you can tell which team it belongs to at a glance. Picking a team folder lands you on the team page with that folder already open.

## Signed-in only

Teams require an account, so this group only appears when you are signed in with teams to search. Guests, and signed-in users with no teams, simply will not see it. The rest of search, your [diagrams and folders](/help/search-panel/the-search-panel/search-diagrams/), works the same either way.

<Tip>
  Searching is often faster than expanding the Teams tree in the Explorer: type a team or folder
  name and jump straight in.
</Tip>
