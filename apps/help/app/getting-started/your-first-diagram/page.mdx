The quickest way to learn livediagram is to build something. This walkthrough takes you from a blank canvas to a small connected diagram. You do not need to sign in, the [canvas always works as a guest](/help/getting-started/guest-vs-account/).

<Figure caption="Every diagram starts from the new-diagram welcome flow: pick Blank or a ready-made template.">
  <NewDiagramWelcome />
</Figure>

## Create a new diagram

<Step n={1}>
  Open [a new diagram](/new). This is livediagram's entry point: the welcome flow where you can
  start from a blank canvas or pick a [template](/help/canvas/templates/) and a
  [theme](/help/canvas/themes/).
</Step>

<Step n={2}>
  Choose **Blank** to follow along from scratch, or pick a template if you would rather start from a
  ready-made layout. Either way you land on the editor with your canvas ready.
</Step>

<Step n={3}>
  Give the diagram a name. Click the title in the header at the top of the editor and type. Renaming
  is instant and saved for you.
</Step>

## Add your first shapes

<Step n={1}>
  Find the **Palette** panel (the floating panel labelled `PALETTE`, in the top-right of the canvas
  by default). The **Shapes** tab is open by default.
</Step>

<Step n={2}>
  **Tap a shape tile** to drop it onto the canvas at its default size, or **drag on the canvas** to
  draw it at the size you want. Try a Square or a Stadium (the pill shape) to start.
</Step>

<Step n={3}>
  **Double-click the shape** to add a label, then type. Press Enter or click away to commit the
  text.
</Step>

<Step n={4}>
  Drop a second shape nearby the same way. If a shape is selected when you add the next one, the new
  shape inherits its size, handy for chaining similarly-sized nodes.
</Step>

For the full set of shapes, devices, and icons you can drop, see the [Palette](/help/palette/) guides.

<Figure caption="Drag a tile from the Palette onto the canvas to drop a shape, which lands selected.">
  <AddingElements />
</Figure>

## Connect them with arrows

<Step n={1}>
  Hover a shape and look for the quick-connect **+** affordances around it. Drag from one to another
  shape to draw an arrow that stays pinned to both.
</Step>

<Step n={2}>
  Prefer to draw freely? Pick the **Arrow** tool from the Palette's **Tools** tab and drag between
  two points.
</Step>

<Step n={3}>
  Select an arrow and right-click it to change its style: straight, curved, or angled, plus line
  thickness, dashes, and arrowhead shape. Double-click an arrow to give it a label.
</Step>

## Keep going

You now have a connected diagram. From here you can:

<Figure caption="A few shapes joined by arrows, with the Palette docked top-right: the editor at a glance.">
  <CanvasOverview />
</Figure>

- **Move and multi-select.** Drag to reposition; drag on empty canvas to marquee-select several elements at once.
- **Recolour everything** with a [theme](/help/canvas/themes/).
- **Share it live.** Hand a colleague a link and they join the same canvas. See [Sharing your diagram](/help/getting-started/sharing-your-diagram/).

<Tip>
  Made a mistake? Undo and redo live at the bottom of the Palette, and the common keyboard shortcuts
  are in [Keyboard essentials](/help/getting-started/keyboard-essentials/).
</Tip>
