The Spotlight mode dims the canvas and spotlights the element under your cursor, so a viewer's attention lands on one thing at a time. Choose it from the mode picker at the top of the floating palette.

## How it works

As you move the cursor, the element beneath it stays bright while everything around it is dimmed. This is useful when you are talking through a diagram and want to focus the room on a single shape before moving on to the next.

</Figure>

## Availability

Spotlight is **desktop only**, and is disabled on an empty canvas. Add an element on a desktop browser and the mode becomes available.

<Tip>
  Spotlight works well alongside the Laser Pointer when presenting: dim the rest of the canvas, then
  point out the detail you want to discuss.
</Tip>
