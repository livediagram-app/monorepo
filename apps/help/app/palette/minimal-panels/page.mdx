Minimal Panels is a toggle, and it is off by default. You will find it behind the gear (settings) icon in the header of the floating palette, which opens a small settings popover. It is only shown when the layout supports it.

<Figure caption="The palette settings popover, with Minimal panels highlighted and switched off.">
  <PaletteSettings highlight="minimal" />
</Figure>

## What it does

When the setting is on, the floating panels (Explorer, Palette, Editor, and AI) are swapped for a compact button bar, a dock. Each panel then opens as a popover from its button instead of floating on the canvas. In the settings popover the hint reads: "Swap floating panels for a compact button bar."

<Figure caption="Floating panels on the left versus the compact dock on the right, where each panel opens as a popover.">
  <MinimalPanels />
</Figure>

## When to use it

Minimal Panels is good for small screens, where floating panels can crowd the canvas, and for anyone who simply prefers a tidier workspace. Tap a button in the dock to open that panel as a popover, then dismiss it to give the canvas back its full width.
