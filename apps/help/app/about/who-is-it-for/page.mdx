livediagram is for teams who think visually and need to do that thinking **together**. A single-player diagramming tool is not the point. The product shines when several people are on the same canvas at once, building one shared diagram in [real time](/help/collaboration/sharing/). That said, it works just as well for one person sketching something out, and the [canvas always works without signing in](/help/getting-started/guest-vs-account/).

## The teams it serves

A few groups feel right at home:

- **Engineering teams** sketching architecture, sequence flows, and system designs. Use [shapes and arrows](/help/palette/shapes-and-arrows/), [technology icons](/help/palette/technology-icons/) for cloud and infrastructure marks, and [tabs](/help/tabs/using-tabs/) to keep related views together.
- **Product and design teams** mapping user journeys, information architecture, and brainstorms with [shapes](/help/palette/shapes-and-arrows/), [text and fonts](/help/customisation/text-and-fonts/), and [themes](/help/customisation/themes/).
- **Cross-functional groups** in workshops, planning sessions, and retrospectives, where everyone needs to contribute at once and see each other's input live.

## Whether you sign in or not

livediagram is friction-free on purpose. A first-time visitor can build a real diagram as a [guest](/help/getting-started/guest-vs-account/) and only later create an account to sync across devices and join [teams](/help/collaboration/teams/). A signed-in user can still hand a share link to a guest who edits without any account at all.
